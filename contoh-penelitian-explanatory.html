explanation text merupakan karangan yang menjelaskan mengapa dan bagaimana sebuah fenomena terjadi.

ciri cirinya :
1. Menjelaskan bagaimana dan mengapa suatu fenomena terjadi.
2. Bagian awal menjelaskan apa yang akan disampaikan dalam teks.
3. Paragraf berikutnya menjelaskan secara berurutan apa yang disampaikan pada bagian awal.
4. Bagian akhir adalah kesimpulan.
5. Menggunakan present tense untuk menjelaskan kebenaran umum. Teks berbentuk non fiksi.

3. Apa yang di maksud dengan interpretative editorial, controversial editorial dan explanatory editorial dan berikan contohnya


1. Interpretative editorial, yang misi utamanya adalah untuk menjelaskan isu atau topik dengan memberikan fakta-fakta dan figur untuk memberikan pengetahuan.

2. Controversial editorial, yang dibuat untuk meyakinkan pembaca pada keinginan atau kepercayaan pada suatu isu. Biasanya dalam editorial jenis ini, pendapat yang berlawanan akan digambarkan lebih buruk.

3. Explanatory editorial, adalah editorial yang menyajikan masalah untuk dinilai para pembaca. Biasanya tujuan teks ini adalah untuk mengidentifikasi masalah, dan membuka mata masyarakat untuk lebih memperhatikan suatu isu.

7. please explanatory text to back of the volcano complete and in accordance with the sequence text structure


The volcano is a powerful force in nature.

First, heat from beneath the earth’s surface melts solid rock into a white hot mixture of gas and a liquid called magma.  The gas-filled magma is lighter than the unmelted rock. This causes the magma to rise toward the surface, melting gaps in the rock as it rises. Then the magma enters the magma chamber. Since a region of magma would be hardened after a past eruption, the magma continues to push and melt its way upward. It pushes through channels of weak, fractured rock consisting of hardened magma. Eventually, the magma and gas push up to the top of the volcano and blast up through an opening called the central vent.
Volcano eruptions can be shocking and dangerous events which may cost lives or cause damage to buildings.
Key:
Red - introductory statement Yellow - technical vocabulary Orange - connectives Green - present tense Blue - third person pronouns Violet - conclusion statement that sums up ideas

11. TASK Students will write an explanatory essay of 500-800 words to respond to the question: "Are we approaching a single, dominant homogenized global culture? Is such a thing desirable?"


Jawaban:

In today's interconnected world, the question of whether we are moving towards a single, dominant homogenized global culture has become a subject of intense debate. As societies and cultures across the globe interact and exchange ideas more than ever before, it is worth examining the potential implications and desirability of such a trend.

The concept of a single, dominant global culture raises both optimistic and cautionary viewpoints. Proponents argue that globalization has led to the proliferation of cultural elements across borders, resulting in a sense of shared experiences and values. This has been facilitated by the widespread dissemination of information through the internet and social media platforms. Advocates of a homogenized global culture posit that it promotes understanding and cooperation among different societies, reduces conflict arising from cultural differences, and allows for the efficient exchange of ideas and technologies.

However, it is essential to consider the potential drawbacks of such cultural homogenization. One significant concern is the potential loss of cultural diversity and identity. As local traditions and customs are overshadowed by global trends, there is a risk of cultural erosion and the dilution of unique practices that define the richness of each society. Moreover, a dominant global culture could lead to the marginalization of smaller or less influential cultures, perpetuating a power imbalance and limiting opportunities for cultural expression.

The desirability of a single, dominant global culture depends on one's perspective and values. Supporters argue that cultural homogenization can promote unity and mutual understanding, fostering a sense of global citizenship. It could also accelerate technological advancements and scientific discoveries by allowing experts to collaborate more seamlessly across borders. Additionally, a shared global culture might facilitate efforts to address pressing global challenges, such as climate change and poverty, through collective action and cooperation.

On the other hand, critics raise concerns about cultural imperialism and the loss of distinct identities. The desire to preserve and protect unique traditions, languages, and ways of life is a powerful sentiment for many communities. They argue that the diversity of cultures enriches humanity's collective experience and contributes to a tapestry of knowledge, creativity, and values. For them, a homogenized global culture could undermine the very essence of what makes each society special.

In conclusion, the question of whether we are moving towards a single, dominant homogenized global culture is complex and multifaceted. While globalization has undoubtedly led to increased cultural interconnectedness, the desirability of such a trend depends on how we balance the benefits of unity and cooperation with the importance of preserving cultural diversity and individuality. Striking this balance requires careful consideration of the potential consequences and a respectful acknowledgment of the diverse perspectives that shape our world. Ultimately, the answer to whether a single global culture is desirable lies in our collective values and aspirations for the future.
